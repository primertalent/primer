## Strategic Thesis

**Build for the world where sourcing is solved.**

AI is collapsing sourcing cost to zero within 18 months. The recruiter who wins is not the one with the most candidates. It is the one who knows which five to run hard, which fifteen to nurture, and which 480 to drop without guilt. Triage. Closing. Memory. Signal.

Today's recruiter wants more candidates. Tomorrow's recruiter has too many and can't work them. Wren is the layer that makes a flooded pipeline actionable.

Every product, marketing, and pricing decision flows from this thesis. If a competitor is racing to commoditize sourcing, Wren is racing to be the layer that survives when sourcing is free.

## ICP

**Primary buyer: the good closer running solo.**

- 5 to 20+ years of recruiting experience
- Solo independent, no team, no coordinator
- Running on LinkedIn Recruiter, spreadsheets, email, maybe Paraform
- No ATS or actively avoiding heavyweight ATS
- Billing $150k to $1M+ annually
- Already competent at the close. Knows their rate. Feels the ceiling on deal volume.

**The pitch is leverage, not coaching.**

"You're already good at this. Wren lets you do it ten times instead of three."

**Why this segmentation matters:**

Bad closers buy Wren as a skill upgrade. They run more deals. Their close rate stays flat. They churn at 60 days because volume didn't fix the underlying gap.

Good closers buy Wren as leverage. They run more deals at their existing close rate. They scale revenue without scaling hours. The floor under their off days and the multiplier on their best days both compound. They stay.

**Where good closers cluster:**

- Paraform's marketplace (high-billing solos with deal flow)
- LinkedIn (founder content, recruiter Slack groups)
- Recruiter newsletters (Recruiting Brainfood, Hung Lee, Tech Recruiter, Boolean Black Belt)
- BountyJobs and similar marketplaces
- Recruiter-only podcasts and Discord servers

**Not the user:**

- In-house TA teams
- Corporate recruiters
- Volume sourcing shops
- Bad closers (will churn, costs more to onboard than they pay)
- Anyone whose primary job is req management instead of closing

## Founder Story

**The line:**

"I spent 15 years as a solo recruiter running on LinkedIn and spreadsheets. I was good at the start of the funnel. I was average at the close. I kept losing candidates I should have won. Wren is what I wish I'd had."

**Why it works:**

- Credibility comes from struggle, not expertise. Every recruiter has lost closes they should have won. Naming it disarms the audience.
- 15 years of being the exact ICP cannot be faked.
- Sets up the contrarian truth without forcing it. The audience leans in to ask "what was breaking?"
- Specific. Not "I'm a recruiter," but "I ran my desk this way for 15 years."

**Where it goes:**

- Hirewren.com hero or about page
- Founder LinkedIn bio: "Helping recruiters close their best candidates with technology"
- Founder LinkedIn manifesto post (draft below)
- Demo opening line
- Investor and partnership conversations

## Two-Layer Messaging

**The principle:** The buyer is not buying truth. They are buying relief from a pain they already feel. Sequence matters.

### Top of funnel — felt pain

Lead here. Cold outreach, ads, homepage hero, LinkedIn posts.

The buyer thinks the problem is volume, not closing. Meet them where they are.

**Lines that work:**

- "More deals from the candidates you already have."
- "Wren finds the deals in your pipeline, runs the close with you, and covers you when you are off."
- "Surfaces deals you missed, multiplies the ones you are running, catches you on the days you are off."
- "More closes from the motion you already run. A floor under your worst weeks."

### Once engaged — contrarian truth

Save for the demo, the discovery call, the manifesto post, the deeper-content channels.

This is where the reframe happens. The buyer realizes their problem was not what they thought.

**Lines that work:**

- "Everyone sold you sourcing tools. No one helped you close."
- "Sourcing tools find candidates. Wren closes them."
- "The elite candidate you waited a year to find deserves more than a Google Doc and instinct."
- "You spent six months sourcing her. Then lost her in the final week. Wren is for that week."

### Don't lead with the contrarian truth on cold outreach

If the buyer hasn't recently lost a close, the message lands abstract. Felt pain converts. Contrarian truth retains.

## Hormozi Four-Indicator Test

Stress test results from $100M Offers framework:

**1. Massive pain.** Mostly yes. Lost deal at the close = $40k+ walking out the door. Pain is sharp and quantifiable per incident. But it is episodic, not constant. Episodic pain is harder to monetize because people forget between deals. Counter: Wren makes the pain visible by surfacing what was missed.

**2. Purchasing power.** Strong yes. Solo recruiters billing $150k+ pay for tools that save one placement per year, easily.

**3. Easy to target.** Strong yes. LinkedIn, Paraform, recruiter newsletters, Slack groups. They cluster and Ryan is one of them.

**4. Growing.** Strong yes. Solo and independent recruiting grew post-2023 layoffs. Tailwind is real.

**Hormozi's harder question — Starving Crowd:**

Are solo recruiters actively searching for a closing tool, or do they think their problem is sourcing?

They think it's sourcing. Wren is not selling to a starving crowd today. The two-layer messaging strategy resolves this. Enter through the door they recognize (leverage, more deals from existing pipeline). Reveal the closing value once they're in.

In 18 months when AI sourcing is commoditized, the same recruiters will be drowning in candidates and unable to convert them. The starving crowd reorients toward closing whether they articulate it or not. Wren is on time, slightly early.

## Objection Handling

**"I do this already and my process is good."**

"Perfect. Upload it. Wren turns your process into the floor, not the ceiling. You stay the closer. Wren makes sure every candidate gets your A game, not your Thursday afternoon game."

**"I already use [ATS / Gem / Paraform]."**

"Keep using it. Wren sits on top. Your ATS stores the record. Wren runs the deal."

**"AI in recruiting is all hype."**

"Most of it is. Wren isn't doing sourcing or outreach automation. Wren does the deal work between the calls you make. If you're losing closes you should be winning, that's what we fix."

**"I don't have time to learn another tool."**

"You paste your candidate, your notes, your JD. Wren does the rest. If it takes more than one motion it's broken. That's the bar."

**"What about data security?"**

"Everything is yours. Your candidates, your notes, your data. We don't sell data, don't share across users, don't train on your records."

**"I'm a solo recruiter, I don't need enterprise tooling."**

"Right. Wren is built for you specifically. No team features, no admin panels. One operator, one desk, one tool that respects your time."

**"What if AI gets so good it just does sourcing for me?"**

"Then you'll have 500 candidates and no way to work them. That's exactly what Wren is built for. The bottleneck is moving from finding candidates to closing them. We're already there."

**"I don't lose that many closes. My close rate is fine."**

"Even great closers leave deals on the table when they're tired, distracted, or running too many at once. Wren isn't here to teach you to close. It's here to make sure your tenth deal of the week gets the same attention as your first."

## Channels and Distribution Plan

> Captured from prior conversations. Update as channels prove out.

**Owned (primary):**

- LinkedIn founder content. Reposition headline to "Helping recruiters close their best candidates with technology." Post 2-3x per week. Mix: tactical recruiting tips, deal desk stories, behind-the-build moments. Goal: 10,000 followers of solo recruiters within 6 months.

**Earned (high leverage):**

- Paraform community. Official partnership long-term. Unofficial first: post in their Discord, refer recruiters who try Wren back to Paraform, build relationships with their team.
- Recruiter newsletters (Hung Lee's Recruiting Brainfood, etc). Reach out with a real story, not a pitch.
- Podcast appearances on recruiter-focused shows.

**Word of mouth (long-term moat):**

- High-billing solo recruiter who tries Wren and tells five others. Most important channel by year two.

**Not pursuing yet:**

- Paid acquisition. Channel-market fit unproven, distribution to ICP is poor on most ad platforms.
- Sales motion. Solo recruiters don't respond to sales motions. They respond to other recruiters.
- PR. Premature. Wait until there's a real story.

## Pricing Position

> Working hypothesis. Validate with first 10 paying users.

**Anchor:** $200/month per user.

**Math:**
- One placement saved per year at $25k average fee = 125x ROI
- Less than one hour of recruiter billable time per month
- API cost per heavy user is $25-80/month with optimization → 60-85% gross margin
- Subscription revenue, not seat-based (one operator, one desk)

**The pitch on pricing:**

"$200 a month. Less than one hour of your billable time. One extra placement a year pays for three years. The AI cost is our problem, not yours."

**What we don't do:**

- Free tier. Devalues the product. ICP can pay.
- Per-seat pricing. Not a team product.
- Usage-based. Confuses the value equation.
- Annual contracts on day one. Build trust monthly first.

**Possible future tiers:**

- Standalone Wren: $200/month
- Wren + agreement parsing + bulk import: $300/month (for recruiters with real history)
- Wren Agency: TBD when team features ever exist (probably never)
