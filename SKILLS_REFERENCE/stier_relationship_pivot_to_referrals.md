# S-Tier Relationship — Pivot to Referrals

**Stage:** 8 — Closing motion (and post-close relationship layer)
**Demonstrates:** Tier-driven cadence, state change capture, the pivot move, referral chain compounding
**Tags:** `tier-system`, `referral-chain`, `relationship-management`

This is the canonical demonstration of the senior recruiter move when an S-tier candidate's timeline shifts mid-process. The placement opportunity closes but the relationship compounds.

---

## The scenario

**Candidate:** Sarah (S-tier)
**Situation:** Recruiter has been running weekly check-ins with Sarah over 2-3 months. She's a strong candidate in active processes.

**State change:** Sarah receives a promotion at her current company. Her timeline for moving shifts significantly — she's no longer an active mover in the near term.

**The pivot move:** Recruiter does not treat this as a loss. The response to a promotion is not to reduce cadence or archive the candidate. It's to congratulate authentically, acknowledge the changed timeline, and make a specific ask: "Do you know anyone in your network who might be ready to make a move?"

**Result:** Sarah provides four warm intros. The relationship compounds beyond the original placement opportunity. Attribution preserved: each intro is linked to Sarah's referral in the system.

---

## What this demonstrates

- **Tier-driven cadence:** S-tier candidates warrant weekly check-ins regardless of active process status. The relationship has standalone value independent of any specific placement.
- **State change capture:** The promotion is a first-class state change — not just a note, but a trigger. Wren updates timeline, adjusts surfacing frequency, and queues the referral ask.
- **The pivot move:** The senior recruiter instinct is to pivot from placement to referral when the placement window closes. Wren codifies this: promotion → referral ask within the same interaction, not a separate workflow.
- **Referral chain compounding:** Four intros from one S-tier relationship. Each intro enters the system with Sarah's referral chain preserved. Future placements from those intros are attributed to Sarah's network, not to cold sourcing.

---

## Build implication

The tier system must include state-change triggers, not just static classifications. A promotion is a trigger for a specific Wren action: update expected timeline, queue referral ask, adjust cadence. The referral ask is not a manual prompt — it's generated by Wren as part of the state change response. The recruiter approves and sends.
