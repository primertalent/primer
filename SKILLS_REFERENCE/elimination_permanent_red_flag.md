# Elimination — Permanent Red Flag

**Demonstrates:** Why eliminations are first-class state, severity tiers, why institutional memory of bad fits matters
**Tags:** `elimination-system`, `red-flag`, `institutional-memory`

This is the canonical case for the elimination system. It shows why eliminations cannot be deletions and why institutional memory of bad fits is as operationally valuable as institutional memory of good ones.

---

## The scenario

**Candidate:** Mike Donovan
**Client:** [Founder-level client — specific role, confidential]

**Sequence of events:**

1. Mike is screened and eliminated for comp misalignment. His stated expectations are materially above what the role can offer. The recruiter closes the process with Mike honestly.

2. Several weeks later, Mike re-engages and claims his comp expectations have changed. He says he's aligned to the role range.

3. Mike is re-introduced to the process and has a call with the founder.

4. During the call, Mike's actual expectations become clear: he had not changed his comp requirements. He had misrepresented his position to re-enter the process.

5. The founder is now aware of the misrepresentation. The recruiter's credibility with the client is affected.

**Outcome:** Mike is permanently eliminated. Not just from this role — from this client and from the recruiter's practice entirely. Severity: 3 (permanent red flag).

---

## What this demonstrates

- **Eliminations are first-class state, not deletions.** If the original elimination record had been deleted or soft-filed, Mike's re-engagement would have appeared as a normal conversation with no prior context. The system must preserve why someone was eliminated, not just that they were.
- **Severity tiers matter.** A comp mismatch alone is a soft pass — surfaces context on future strong matches. A pattern of misrepresentation that affects a client relationship is a permanent red flag that travels across all clients.
- **Institutional memory of bad fits.** The recruiter's credibility with the founder was affected because the re-engagement happened without the elimination reason surfaced at the right moment. Wren's job is to surface that reason before any re-engagement conversation begins — not after.

---

## Build implication

The elimination system needs three severity tiers:
- **Soft pass:** Comp mismatch, timing mismatch, fit gap on one role. Surfaces context on future matches.
- **Hard pass:** Pattern of flakiness, multiple comp misalignments, cultural red flags. Surfaces as warning on any re-engagement.
- **Permanent red flag:** Misrepresentation, breach of trust, conduct affecting client relationships. Triggers active suppression — Wren surfaces the flag before any re-introduction conversation, not after.

Client-specific eliminations stay scoped to that client. Severity-3 flags travel across all clients with reason context preserved.
